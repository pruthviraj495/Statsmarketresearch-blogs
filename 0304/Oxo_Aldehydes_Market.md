 Oxo aldehydes, also known as aldehyde oxo compounds or aldehyde olefin products, are a class of chemical compounds produced through the oxo process (also called hydroformylation). The oxo process involves the reaction of olefins (alkenes) with synthesis gas (a mixture of carbon monoxide and hydrogen) in the presence of a catalyst, typically cobalt or rhodium complexes. This reaction leads to the formation of aldehydes, which are important intermediates in the synthesis of various chemicals.</p><p>
