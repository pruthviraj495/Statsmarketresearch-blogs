The coolant is brought into and out of the radiator by the cooling hose. There are two cooling hoses, one placed above the radiator and the other placed below the radiator. A coolant is passed through the tubes of the radiator hose to cool down the engine and dissipate the excess heat. The hot coolant then passes through the inlet tank of the radiator, generally located on the top of the radiator, it then passes through the tubes of the radiator. As the coolant passes through the tubes of the radiator, it dissipates the excess heat and thus maintains the optimal efficiency of the engine.</p>
