 <p>Automobile out-wheel motor is a motor installed next to the wheel and drives one wheel alone, with a motor and a reducer on both sides. This design cancels the main reducer and differential, so the overall power consumption is relatively small. Different from the hub motor, the hub motor is the motor embedded in the wheel, the stator is fixed on the tire, and the rotor is fixed on the axle instead of transmitting power to the wheel through the transmission shaft.</p>
